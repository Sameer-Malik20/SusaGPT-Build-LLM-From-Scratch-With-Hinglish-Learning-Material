# Top 100 Security Engineer Interview Questions (2026 Edition)

## 2. Deep Technical Categories
- **Core Fundamentals**: Networking, OS, Cryptography.
- **Application Security**: OWASP Top 10, Secure SDLC.
- **Offensive Security**: Pen-testing, Exploits, Social Engineering.
- **Cloud Security**: Shared Responsibility, IAM, AWS/Azure/GCP.
- **Incident Response**: Forensic, Malware, Crisis Management.
- **AI/LLM Security**: Prompt Injection, Poisoning, Guardrails.

## 3. Attack Flow Diagrams
**The 'Interview' Strategy (STAR Method):**
```mermaid
graph TD
    Q[Question: Tell me about a time you found a bug] --> S[Situation: Describe the app/context]
    S --> T[Task: What was your job?]
```

## 4. Top 10 High-Frequency Questions (Samples)
1. **Explain the 3-way handshake and how it relates to a SYN flood attack.**
2. **What is the difference between Symmetric and Asymmetric encryption?**
3. **Walk me through the steps you would take if you discovered an active ransomware attack.**
4. **How do you secure a 'Public' S3 bucket without making the app fail?**
5. **What is 'Prompt Injection' and how would you defend against it in a production LLM?**
6. **Explain 'Zero Trust' architecture in 3 simple sentences.**
7. **What is the difference between SAST, DAST, and SCA?**
8. **How does a 'Buffer Overflow' work and how do you prevent it?**
9. **Explain 'SAML' vs 'OIDC' for Single Sign-On.**
10. **If you have $100,000 for a security budget, where do you spend it first?**

---

## 5. Defensive Mitigation Strategies (for Interviews)
- **Don't Lie**: If you don't know the answer, say: "I don't know the exact answer, but based on my knowledge of X, I think it works like Y."
- **Think Out Loud**: The interviewer wants to see "How you think," not just the final answer.
- **Ask Clarifying Questions**: "Is this for a web app or a mobile app?", "Is the database on-premise or in the cloud?".

---

## 6. Failure Cases
- **The 'Silent' Candidate**: Not talking for 2 minutes while you think. (Say: 'Give me a second to think through this').
- **Giving 'Vague' Answers**: "I would just use a firewall." (Bad). "I would use a WAF to block L7 attacks and an IPS for L4..." (Good).

---

## 7. Debugging and Investigation Guide
- **Glassdoor**: Search for specific company names (e.g., 'Google Security Interview') to see what they actually ask.
- **Mock Interviews**: Practice with a friend or use AI (like ChatGPT) to "Roleplay" an interviewer.
- **LeetCode (Security)**: Some companies ask for basic coding/scripting tests (e.g., "Write a Python script to parse a log file").

## 9. Security Best Practices
- **Master the 'Basics'**: 80% of candidates fail because they don't know the difference between 'Encoding,' 'Hashing,' and 'Encryption.'
- **Portfolio over Talk**: If you have a project on GitHub that solves the problem they are asking about, tell them!

---

## 10. Production Hardening Techniques
- **Whiteboarding**: Practice drawing your security architectures on a whiteboard or a digital tool (like Excalidraw).
- **Situation-Action-Result**: Always end your answer by showing the "Positive Impact" of your work.

---

## 11. Monitoring and Logging Considerations
- **Follow-up Questions**: Always have 2-3 questions for the interviewer at the end (e.g., "What is the biggest security challenge your team is facing right now?").

---

## 12. Common Mistakes
- **Being 'Arrogant'**: No matter how good you are, nobody wants to work with someone who thinks they know everything.
- **Forgetting the 'Business'**: Answering like a hacker instead of a business professional. (Remember: Security is about protecting money and trust).

---

## 13. Compliance Implications
- **Ethics**: If they ask "Would you hack a competitor for us?", the answer is always **NO**. This is a test of your ethics.

---

## 14. Interview Practice (The 'Homework')
1. Record yourself answering: "Tell me about yourself." (It should be <2 minutes).
2. Write a 1-page summary of your favorite security project.
3. Learn the 'OWASP Top 10' by heart.

---

## 15. Latest 2026 Security Patterns and Threats
- **The 'AI-Assisted' Interview**: Interviewers using AI to check if your answers are copied from the internet.
- **Live Coding (Python/Rego)**: Being asked to write a security policy (Rego) or a log parser during the call.
- **Behavioral Questions**: "Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a developer about a security bug."
